First Time Rower, First Time SEA Games Contender

Van Adrian Maxilom, a former basketball player of CYS (Clan Youthful School) has made a tremendous breakthrough in his athletic career. A lot of his notable achievements were being a part of the WCSU (West Cavite Sports Unit) Men's basketball team and won silver, joined the DICES (Diocese of Imus Catholic Education System) which they also won, and then he became part of a 3-peat championship (won 3 times) back in his Senior Highschool days. Fast-forward today, he strengthened his body during the pandemic in their own gym and then after that, he started doing trainings with the Philippine Rowing team. Then with their continuous training, he eventually got to be a part of the national team and became one of the qualified members of the Philippine rosters in the Men's Rowing team in the upcoming SEA Games this May 2022 in Hanoi, Vietnam together with Tokyo Olympian Cris Niervarez. What is even more impressive is that this will be his FIRST competition in rowing.

FareWell director

       Award-winning director Eduardo has passed away. He was only 41 years old. According to a statement sent by his sister Clara to PEP.ph, Eduardo died at St. Luke’s Medical Center in Quezon City on Feb. 21. “It is with great sorrow that we announce the passing of Eduardo,” said her statement. “He has been diagnosed with lymphoma last year.” A wake will be held Feb. 24 - 26 at Arlington Memorial Chapels in Quezon City. Eduardo is known for films such as “Kid's Home” “Palitan” “Grandmother Igna” “Ordinary Family” and “Decent guys” for which he won as Cinemalaya best director in 2019.
